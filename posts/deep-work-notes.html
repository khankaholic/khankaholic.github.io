Deep Work Notes for Engineers

Focus is not a personality trait. It is a system. Most engineers already know what deep work is; the real challenge is keeping it intact after Slack pings and meeting churn.

Protect time at the calendar level

If focus blocks are not visible on your calendar, they will be consumed by urgent but shallow tasks. My baseline is two 90-minute blocks each week day, booked as non-negotiable production time.

Cut decision load

Start each block with one exact objective: a function to rewrite, a test to stabilize, a document to finish. Ambiguous goals waste the first 20 minutes.

Share output, not hours

Teams care about progress and clarity. I post concise end-of-day notes: what shipped, what changed, and what is blocked. This keeps collaboration healthy without constant context switching.
